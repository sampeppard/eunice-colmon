PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT COACHING


OVERVIEW

When you receive repeated complaints from clients or staff about a manager's interpersonal relationships, inability to make decisions, lack of organization skills, inability to develop and/or leverage team talent, or other behaviors that lead to lowered morale and productivity, what you may need is performance improvement coaching.

Eunice eases the departmental leader’s concerns when you have a problem, may have had failed attempts at correcting the problem, and may be running out of time or patience.

Process & Experience

A coaching engagement begins with a sponsor who needs a direct report to change a challenging behavior. Once we set clear goals, the sponsor articulates this intent to the coaching client, secures agreement to the proposed engagement, and is kept apprised of the coaching client's progress.

Throughout the confidential process, Eunice partners with each coaching client to explore internal and external forces that are affecting self awareness, behavior, perceptions of others, and professional reputation. With guided intervention, encouragement, and proven practical instruction, Eunice and the coaching client arrive at solutions, meet stated goals, and enable real professional growth.

Positive results are imminent when you have a coach who provides an unbiased ear; responds with a confident, candid voice; offers a balance of rational, enlightening, actionable insights; and instills accountability for change. Eunice is that coach. She helps coaching clients 'want' to become better communicators, managers, and leaders, and ultimately, reach their goals.


Benefits & Outcomes

Clients who actively participate in their coaching program leave with:

  • Openness to blind spots—increased self awareness
  • Heightened level of social awareness and inclusive behaviors
  • Increased sense of ownership to change and improvement
  • Enhanced listening skills and better ability to resolve conflict with an impartial, proactive mindset
  • More flexible approach to addressing people, problems, procedures and environment
  • Truer connection to their role in your organization and increased work satisfaction
  • Stronger commitment to meeting and exceeding expectations


PROGRAMS

Each Performance Improvement Coaching Program is designed to meet the needs of the sponsor and the coaching client, and structured utilizing the following series of interactions:



  • Introductory Meeting with Coaching Client in Person
  • Tailored Coaching Sessions via Telephone
  • Onsite Shadowing (when required)
  • Mid-point Update with Sponsor via Telephone
  • Final Evaluation with Sponsor via Telephone


Below is a select list of key program options, all designed to improve staff relationships and productivity:



INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTOR (2-4 Month Program


Coaching Focal Points Include:

  • Internalizing assessment results (e.g., DISC, Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator, Social Styles)
  • Heightening self-awareness of non-verbal behaviors and their impact on others
  • Identifying and fulfilling role and contribution expectations
  • Adapting to workplace culture and environment
  • Developing time management, stress management, and organizational skills
  • Assessing specific performance gaps and developing strategies for attainment


MANAGER (3-6 Month Program)


Coaching Focal Points Include:

  • Internalizing assessment results (e.g., DISC, Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator, Social Styles)
  • Increasing competence in foundational management skills
  • Strengthening direct report relationships
  • Managing up – Defining, accepting, and meeting manager’s expectations
  • Improving influencing skills with internal and external stakeholders (non-report relationships)
  • Leading resistant employees through change
  • Setting and managing priorities while structuring and organizing workload


EXECUTIVES (6-9 Month Program)


Coaching Focal Points Include:

  • Internalizing assessment results (e.g., DISC, Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator, Social Styles)
  • Coaching direct reports on leadership
  • Establishing clear roles and responsibilities for direct reports
  • Aligning priorities with departmental strategies
  • Driving change with vision, purpose, and patience
  • Applying advanced management and leadership skills
  • Managing Up – Defining, accepting, and meeting manager’s (sponsor’s) expectations
  • Managing anger, disappointment, and expectations; Increasing emotional intelligence
